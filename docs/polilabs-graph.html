How to read this

Every bill in the corpus is shredded into this shape. A Bill is the bibliographic record; it HAS_VERSION a BillVersion (a dated text snapshot); the version HAS_SECTION a tree of Section nodes (PARENT_OF links the nesting). Mechanical extractors then read the section text and add the substantive layer: a section DEFINES a DefinedTerm, CITES_EXTERNAL a StatuteSection of existing US Code, or AMENDS an AmendmentOperation that TARGETS a StatuteSection.

The point of the graph is the cross-bill links. Here, H.R. 6881's definition of “artificial intelligence” does not restate the term — it points BY_REFERENCE to 15 U.S.C. 9401, the definition Congress enacted in 2020. Meanwhile H.R. 8516 AMENDS that very same statute section, inserting new defined terms into it. Two unrelated-looking bills are in fact bound to the same piece of law — a connection a keyword search would never surface, but a graph traversal finds in one hop.

Not every node type is drawn here. The graph also carries cosponsorship and a provenance spine (Extractor → ProvenanceRecord) recording which parser produced each derived edge. See the PDF for the full schema.
